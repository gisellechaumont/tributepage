Ada Lovelace

The First Computer Programmer

Here's a time line of Lovelace's life:

  • 1815 - Born in London, England
  • 1824 - She recieved the best education about maths, french and music.
  • 1829 - She was paralyzed after a bout of measles
  • 1831 - She was able to walk with crutches. Despite the illnesses, she developed her mathematical and technological skills.
  • 1833 - Lovelace became close friends with her tutor Mary Somerville, who introduced her to Charles Babbage in 1833.
  • 1835 - She got married with William King-Noel
  • 1838 - When her husband inherited the noble title, he became the Earl of Lovelace and Ada became the Countess of Lovelace. That's why she is known as Ada Lovelace.
  • 1843 - Publication of Ada's Notes in the scientific journal "Scientific Memoirs"
  • 1852 - Die in Marylebone, London, England
“The more I study, the more insatiable do I feel my genius for it to be.” - A.L.
